# Code of conduct

Meanwhile is built through direct technical disagreement, careful review, and shared responsibility for software that executes untrusted code. Participation requires rigor without hostility.

## Expected behavior

- Critique designs, evidence, and code rather than people.
- State assumptions, tradeoffs, and uncertainty plainly.
- Give specific, actionable review feedback and respond to the strongest version of an argument.
- Respect different backgrounds, identities, experience levels, communication styles, and time zones.
- Protect private repositories, prompts, credentials, customer information, vulnerability details, and unpublished provider data.
- Credit others' work and disclose relevant provenance or conflicts of interest.
- Accept correction, repair harm, and help leave the project clearer than you found it.

Technical authority comes from evidence and stewardship, not status, volume, or persistence.

## Unacceptable behavior

- Harassment, discrimination, threats, stalking, sexualized attention, or demeaning language.
- Personal attacks, intimidation, ridicule, or repeated bad-faith argument.
- Publishing another person's private information without permission.
- Sharing secrets, private source, raw prompts, provider identifiers, security reports, or customer data.
- Deliberately disrupting discussions, reviews, releases, infrastructure, or provider resources.
- Retaliating against a reporter, reviewer, maintainer, or person raising a safety concern.
- Using project access, community position, or generated content to impersonate or misrepresent another person.

## Scope

This policy applies in repository issues, pull requests, reviews, discussions, chats, community events, and public spaces where someone represents Meanwhile. It also applies to private conduct that materially threatens participant safety or the project's ability to operate.

## Reporting

Report conduct concerns privately to the maintainers. While the project has no separate moderation inbox, use GitHub's private **Report a vulnerability** form for this repository and begin the title with `Conduct report:`. Reports through that route are handled as confidential moderation matters, not security advisories.

If the private form is unavailable, open a minimal issue asking maintainers to enable a private contact channel. Do not include names, screenshots, private messages, or incident details publicly.

A report should include what happened, where and when it happened, relevant context, the requested immediate safety measure, and whether anyone faces ongoing risk. Share only material necessary for review.

Anyone named in a report will not participate in its initial handling. Maintainers will limit disclosure to people needed to investigate and respond, while recognizing that complete anonymity cannot always be guaranteed.

## Enforcement

Maintainers evaluate context, severity, pattern, impact, and participant safety. Responses may include:

1. a private clarification or request to correct behavior;
2. removal or editing of harmful content;
3. a formal warning with conditions for continued participation;
4. temporary restriction from project spaces or responsibilities;
5. permanent removal and revocation of project access;
6. escalation to the hosting platform or appropriate authorities when necessary for safety.

Good-faith reports and respectful technical disagreement are not violations. Knowingly false reports, retaliation, and weaponized process may themselves require enforcement.

Maintainers should document decisions privately, communicate the outcome to affected parties where appropriate, and apply the policy consistently. This policy may be refined as the community grows; material changes are recorded in [CHANGELOG.md](CHANGELOG.md).
